HOW TO COOK OKONOMIYAKI

HERE'S THE INGREDIENTS



HOW TO PREPARE

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (120 g)
    • ;
  • ¼ tsp salt (kosher or sea salt; use half if using table salt)
    • r
  • ¼ tsp sugar
  • ¼ tsp baking powder
  • 2-3 inch Nagaimo/Yamaimo (5-8 cm, 160 g, 5.6 oz)
  • ¾ cup dashi (180 ml)
  • 1 large cabbage head (1.6 lb or 740 g)
  • ½ lb sliced pork belly (227 g)
  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ cup Tenkasu/Agedama (tempura scraps) (8 Tbsp)
  • ¼ cup pickled red ginger (beni shoga or kizami beni shoga) (4 Tbsp)
  • neutral flavor oil (vegetable, canola, etc)

    • Okonomiyaki Sauce

  • 1 ½ Tbsp sugar
  • 2 Tbsp oyster sauce
  • 4 Tbsp ketchup
  • 3 ½ Tbsp Worcestershire sauce

    • Toppings

  • Japanese mayonnaise
  • Katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes)
  • Aonori (dried green seaweed)
  • Green onions/scallions
  • pickled red ginger (beni shoga or kizami beni shoga)

INSTRUCTION

  1. Gather all the ingredients.

    2. Prepare Okonomiyaki Batter

  2. In a large bowl, combine 1 cup (120 g) all-purpose flour, ¼ tsp. salt, ¼ tsp. sugar, and ¼ tsp. baking powder and mix all together.
  3. Peel and grate nagaimo in a small bowl. It can get itchy, so work quickly and rinse your hand right after. Nagaimo is very slimy and slippery, so make sure you have a good grip if you wear a glove.
  4. Add the grated nagaimo and dashi in the bowl.
  5. Mix all together till combined. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least one hour.

    6. Okonomiyaki Sauce Ingredients

  6. To make homemade Okonomiyaki Sauce, combine 1 ½ Tbsp. sugar, 2 Tbsp. oyster sauce, 4 Tbsp. ketchup, and 3 ½ Tbsp. Worcestershire sauce in a small bowl. Mix all together until sugar is completely dissolved.

    7. Cook Okonomiyaki

  7. Remove the core of the cabbage and mince it. Set aside to let the moisture evaporate so it won’t dilute the batter.
  8. Cut the pork belly slices in half and set aside
  9. Take out the batter from the refrigerator and add 4 large eggs, ½ cup (8 Tbsp.) tempura scraps (Tenkasu/Agedama), and ¼ cup (4 Tbsp.) pickled red ginger (Kizami Beni Shoga) in the bowl. Mix well until well-combined.
  10. Add chopped cabbage to the batter 1/3 at a time. Mix well before adding the rest.
  11. In a large pan, heat vegetable oil on medium heat. When the frying pan is hot (400F/200C), spread the batter in a circle on the pan. We like thicker okonomiyaki (final thickness is ¾ inches (2 cm)). If you’re new to making okonomiyaki, make a smaller and thinner size so it’s easier to flip.
  12. Place 2-3 sliced pork belly on top of Okonomiyaki and cook covered for 5 minutes.
  13. When the bottom side is nicely browned, flip over.
  14. Gently press the okonomiyaki to fix the shape and keep it together. Cover and cook for another 5 minutes.
  15. Flip over one last time and cook uncovered for 2 minutes. If you’re going to cook next batch, transfer to a plate.

    16. Serving & Preserving

    Here are the ingredients for toppings. Apply okonomiyaki sauce with brush, add Japanese mayonnaise in zigzagging lines (optional), and sprinkle dried bonito flakes (katsuobushi). You can also put dried green seaweed (aonori), chopped green onions, and pickled red ginger on top for garnish.
